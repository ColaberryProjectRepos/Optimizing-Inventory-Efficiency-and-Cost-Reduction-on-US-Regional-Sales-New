Solution Impact:

Strategic investments in two key areas—inventory optimization and targeted marketing efforts—have delivered substantial returns, each addressing distinct aspects of operational efficiency and profitability:

Inventory Optimization (Case 1 - WARE-NMK1003): Implementing inventory optimization strategies has resulted in an 11% ROI by reducing holding costs. By decreasing the order quantity by 10%, the company saves $136,000 annually. This not only improves cost efficiency but also enhances inventory management, ensuring better stock turnover and minimizing excess inventory, which leads to lower overall operating costs.

Targeted Marketing Efforts (Case 2 - WARE-NBV1002): The investment in targeted marketing initiatives has generated a 16% annual ROI. By increasing marketing spend by 15%, the company boosts sales by 10%, resulting in $686,748 in additional revenue and $247,229 in increased profit. This demonstrates the financial advantage of investing in marketing to drive sales growth, particularly in underperforming regions, while aligning with the goal of improving inventory turnover and customer demand satisfaction.
